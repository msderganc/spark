# Scoring (N / U / E)

Every shortlist item receives three advisory scores (1–5 each):

- **N** — Novelty
- **U** — Uniqueness (estimated familiarity)
- **E** — Elegance (mode-relative)

Display as `N/U/E` (e.g. `4/3/4`). Scores **never** auto-declare a winner; human **`lock:N`** owns the final choice.

---

## Novelty (N)

*Surprising for **this Frame** vs path-of-least-resistance (POLR) — not vs "creativity" in the abstract.*

| Score | Rubric |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Stock / cliché for this ask |
| **2** | Slight twist on the obvious |
| **3** | Clear alternative category; still familiar pattern |
| **4** | Surprising for this Frame; breaks usual categories |
| **5** | Reframes how you'd even approach the ask |

Steer axis **I** (Innovation) pushes generation toward more or less innovative *direction*; **N** scores the result against POLR for the current Frame.

---

## Uniqueness (U)

*Estimated **familiarity under available context** — not "never seen on Earth" as fact.*

| Score | Rubric |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Extremely common; seen everywhere |
| **2** | Common pattern with minor variation |
| **3** | Uncommon but recognizable precedent exists |
| **4** | Rare; hard to point to a clear existing twin |
| **5** | No known close analogue *(claim, not proof)* |

### Grounding & unverified

- When **`ground:`** was **not** used (default), mark U as **unverified** (or show confidence: low / med / high).
- Do not present U=5 as verified originality without grounding evidence.
- **U ≠ N:** an idea can be novel-for-Frame (high N) yet familiar in the wild (low U), or rare (high U) yet obvious given the Frame (low N).

v1 has no U steer axis; push uniqueness via free-text or indirect steers.

---

## Elegance (E) — dual rubrics

Pick **one** rubric based on active mode. Do not mix.

### Elegance — Ideate

*Clear, coherent, low-waste fit to Frame.*

| Score | Rubric |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Incoherent, muddled, or impossible to explain |
| **2** | Overstuffed / many unrelated pieces |
| **3** | Adequate; some fluff or fuzzy edges |
| **4** | Clear, coherent, easy to explain; little waste |
| **5** | Crisp concept; almost nothing extra; snaps to the Frame |

### Elegance — Solve

*Solves well with minimal complexity.*

| Score | Rubric |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Doesn't really solve it / Rube Goldberg |
| **2** | Works but heavy, fragile, or overbuilt |
| **3** | Adequate solution; some unnecessary parts |
| **4** | Strong fit; lean; few moving parts |
| **5** | Solves cleanly; almost nothing extra |

---

## Anti-inflation

- **Not all 5s.** A healthy shortlist spans the scale; clustering at 4–5 means scores are useless.
- **U=5 is rare** and must be hedged even when assigned.
- **Naming / title-only Frames:** default most items to **U ≤ 3**. Reserve U=4 for unusual coinages with clear precedent doubt; U=5 only with an explicit hedge ("claim, not proof"). Prefer spreading N/E instead of stacking high U.
- After **`parallel:`** merge/dedupe: **re-score** the converged shortlist (do not keep persona-local inflated U).
- Killed items should read as genuine fixation — if everything scores high, recalibrate against POLR.
- Elaborate 2–3 strong picks; do **not** crown the highest total.
- Scores inform the human; they do not replace judgment or **`lock:N`**.

---

## Novel ∧ appropriate (context)

An idea "counts" when it is both surprising-for-Frame (**N**) and fit to constraints (**E**). High N alone is novelty theater; high E alone is safe but boring. The human balances both at lock time.
